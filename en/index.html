Sigillo Italiano

Recognizing what preserves authenticity, method, tradition, and integrity.

Sigillo Italiano is an Italian-Brazilian cultural curation devoted to distinguishing products, producers, and initiatives whose Italianness manifests itself coherently, without promotional staging and without relying on commercial validation.

Institutional note

Sigillo Italiano is neither an award, nor a marketplace, nor a technical certification. Its role is to recognize what preserves cultural truth, historical depth, and integrity of method amid the excess of simulated Italianness.

The site exists as a cultural institution, editorial archive, and manifesto of criteria. Its form should remain quiet enough for recognition, rather than promotion, to stay at the center.

Four principles

01

Authenticity

A truthful relation to the knowledge, repertoires, and languages of Italian culture.

02

Method

Discipline of execution, technical rigor, and formal permanence in the act of making.

03

Tradition

Living continuity with a cultural inheritance, free of folklore or decorative simulation.

04

Integrity

Coherence among origin, materiality, conduct, discourse, and public presence.

Inaugural edition

In 2026, Sigillo Italiano will grant only seven recognitions. None will be obtained through application. The curation identifies, researches, and decides independently.

The recognized recipients may use Sigillo Italiano on their labels or in their institutional communication, should they wish, as a sign of authenticity and cultural quality.
